THUS SPOKE ZARATHUSTRA

Category : Philosophy/Literature

Author : Friedrich Nietzsche

SYNOPSIS :

"Thus Spoke Zarathustra" by Friedrich Nietzsche is a profound philosophical novel published between 1883 and 1885. Through the character of Zarathustra, Nietzsche presents his ideas on the Übermensch (Overman), eternal recurrence, and the death of God, among other existential themes. The Übermensch represents the ideal of self-overcoming and transcending societal norms, while eternal recurrence suggests life as an eternal cycle repeating infinitely. The novel also explores the decline of traditional religious beliefs and the necessity for individuals to create their own values in a world without God. "Thus Spoke Zarathustra" continues to influence existentialist thought and provoke philosophical discourse.
